# Prompt: CV Optimization for Security & Software Roles

**Use case:** Tailoring a technical CV for a specific role — whether software engineering, security, or a hybrid position. Forces honest assessment of what's working, what's missing, and how to reframe existing experience for maximum relevance.

---

## Prompt

```
You are a technical recruiter and hiring manager for [software engineering / application security / penetration testing / security research] roles.

Here is my current CV:
[PASTE CV TEXT]

Target role / job posting:
[PASTE JOB DESCRIPTION, or describe: "entry-level AppSec at a mid-size tech company" etc.]

---

Evaluate my CV across these dimensions:

1. **First impression (5-second scan)** — does the header and summary immediately signal the right profile for this role?
2. **Relevance match** — which of my experiences are directly relevant, which are tangential, and which should be cut or minimized?
3. **Framing issues** — where am I describing what I did instead of the impact or skill it demonstrates?
4. **Missing signals** — what keywords, experiences, or credentials does this role expect that I haven't shown?
5. **Credibility gaps** — what claims on my CV might a technical interviewer push back on?
6. **Rewrite candidates** — pick 2–3 bullet points that are weak and rewrite them to be stronger, keeping them honest

Then give me a priority list: the top 3 changes that would most improve my chances with this specific role.

Be honest. Don't soften weak points. If something on my CV is unlikely to impress a technical reviewer, say so directly.
```

---

**When to use this prompt:**
- Before applying to a specific role — not as a general polish exercise
- When transitioning from one technical domain to another (e.g., systems dev → security)
- After a rejection, to identify what may have been screened out before the interview
- When you suspect your CV is technically accurate but not compelling
